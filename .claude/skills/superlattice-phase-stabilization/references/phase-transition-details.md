# Phase Transition Details

## Coordination Number Changes

### Tetrahedral to Body-Centered Tetragonal
- **Initial state**: Open tetrahedral structure
  - Coordination number: 4
  - Typical materials: GaAs, ZnS, CdTe
  
- **Final state**: Body-centered tetragonal structure
  - Coordination number: 6
  - Volume reduction: 15-20%

## Band Structure Effects

### Band Gap Reduction
The high-pressure phase typically shows:
- Dramatic band gap reduction
- Possible transition to metallic behavior
- Changes in effective mass

### Volume Reduction Mechanism
The 15-20% volume reduction occurs due to:
1. Increased atomic packing efficiency
2. Change in bond angles
3. Reduced interatomic distances

## Hysteresis and Metastability

### Hysteresis Loop
- Forward transformation pressure: P1
- Reverse transformation pressure: P2 < P1
- Hysteresis width: ΔP = P1 - P2

### Metastable Retention Conditions
1. Individual layers must be thin enough
2. Strain energy must compensate for bulk free energy difference
3. Interface coherence must be maintained
