# Metal-Ion Interstitial Analysis

## Identify the Interstitial Type

Determine the type of interstitial present in the semiconductor structure:

1. **Cation interstitials**: Extrinsic metal atoms in interstitial positions
2. **Self-interstitials**: Intrinsic atoms of the host material in interstitial sites
3. **Specific cases**: Li in Si (a common example of interstitial donors)

## Determine Donor Behavior

Evaluate whether the interstitial acts as a donor:

1. **General rule**: Metal-ion interstitials typically behave as donors
2. **Mechanism**: The metal atom prefers to donate its valence electron(s) to the conduction band
3. **Exception**: Self-interstitials in elemental semiconductors act as deep centers

## Assess Hydrogen-like Properties

Check if hydrogen-like description applies:

1. **Applies when**: No counterpart exists to form a charge-compensating bond
2. **Key characteristics**:
   - Higher excited states resemble classical substitutional donors
   - Ground state shows significant chemical shift
3. **Ground state level**: Depends on the specific incorporation site

## Evaluate Incorporation Factors

Consider factors affecting interstitial formation and observation:

1. **Ionic size**: Ionized cations are smaller than anions, making interstitial incorporation easier
2. **Observation**: Interstitial donors are more readily observed than interstitial acceptors
3. **Site dependence**: Ground state energy levels vary based on incorporation site

## Characterize the Defect

Provide final characterization based on analysis:

- **Donor classification**: Identify as shallow or deep center
- **Energy level**: Specify ground state position relative to band edges
- **Charge state**: Determine ionization state under relevant conditions
