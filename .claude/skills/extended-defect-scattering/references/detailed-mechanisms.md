# Detailed Scattering Mechanisms

## Dislocation Scattering

### Edge Dislocation Disturbance
Edge dislocations present major disturbance due to:
1. **Surrounding stress field**: Creates a deformation potential that affects carriers
2. **Charging of reconstructed core states**: Core states become electrically active

### Core State Physics
- Core states exist in the bandgap of semiconductors
- Their energy level position relative to Fermi level determines charge state
- States are screened by free carriers surrounding the dislocation
- Screening length is the Debye length (L_D)

### Piezoelectric Contributions
In piezoelectric crystals, strain fields around both edge and screw dislocations induce charges.
**References**: Levinson (1965), Podor (1977)

## Temperature Dependence

### Core Charge States in Ge and Si
- **Low temperature**: Core states are positively charged
- **High temperature**: Core states become negatively charged
- **Neutrality temperature**: Exists where core charge transitions sign

### Mobility at Neutrality
Even when core is neutral at the transition temperature:
- Mobility in deformed Ge is still lower than undeformed Ge
- This proves strain field scattering exists independently of core charge

## Anisotropy Theory

### Refined Theory (Düster and Labusch 1973)
Considers:
- Deformation potential influence
- Anisotropic nature of dislocation strain fields
- Preferred orientation of dislocations

### Experimental Confirmation
- **Schroter (1969)**: Demonstrated anisotropic mobility
- **Podor (1974)**: Confirmed directional dependence

### Mobility Ratios
For aligned dislocation arrays:
- μ_parallel ≈ μ_undeformed (minimal scattering along dislocation line)
- μ_perpendicular ≪ μ_undeformed (strong scattering perpendicular to dislocation array)

## Defect Cluster Classification

### Type 1: Defect Associates
- Small clusters of point defects
- Behavior depends on:
  - Effective cross-section size
  - Charge state after trapping

### Type 2: Different Phase Inclusions
- Small inclusions of different phase within host material
- Charge sources:
  - Carrier trapping at interface states
  - Difference in electron affinity between phases

### Scattering Regimes
1. **Point-defect-like**: Clusters much smaller than mean free path
2. **Intermediate**: Partial screening, extended scattering cross-section
3. **Carrier repulsion**: Clusters comparable to or larger than mean free path

## Theoretical Derivations

### Scattering Cylinder Treatment
The Podor (1966) formula treats:
- Dislocation as a scattering cylinder
- Analogous to spherical scattering center treatment
- Accounts for cylindrical geometry in scattering cross-section

### Space Charge Region
For charged defects/clusters:
- Space charge extends into surrounding material
- Extent: up to several Debye lengths
- Carriers are repelled/attracted before reaching defect core

### Screening Effects
- Free carriers screen the charged defect's Coulomb potential
- Screening strength increases with carrier concentration
- At high doping, extended defect scattering becomes less significant
