# Persistent Photoconductivity Mechanism

Analyze and explain the physical mechanism behind persistent photoconductivity (PPC) in semiconductors at low temperatures.

## When to Use
- Photoconductivity persists for days or months after optical excitation ends
- Low temperature conditions (typically < 100K)
- Materials with activators showing extremely small capture cross-sections
- Specific materials: CdTe:Cl, Al_xGa_{1-x}As with D-X centers

## Phenomenon Identification

**Key Observation:** At low temperatures, photoconductivity persists indefinitely after optical excitation is terminated.

## Analysis Steps

### 1. Carrier Maintenance Mechanism

- Initial generated carriers are extracted from the photoconductor
- To maintain neutrality, carriers are **repeatedly replenished**
- **Recombination is extremely slow**, preventing carrier depletion

### 2. Capture Cross-Section Analysis

Typical values can be smaller than 10^-30 cm²:
- **CdTe:Cl at 77K**: Lifetime > 10^6 hours
- **Al_xGa_{1-x}As with D-X centers**: Similar extreme lifetimes

### 3. Barrier Formation (Physical Cause)

**Giant Lattice Relaxation:**
- Large impurity-lattice coupling causes substantial lattice deformation
- Example: 1.2 eV relaxation in AlGaAs when impurity charge state changes

**Resulting Barrier:**
- Lattice relaxation creates a large effective barrier for carrier capture
- At low temperatures, **thermal excitation cannot overcome this barrier**
- **Tunneling probability** through barrier is sufficiently small to explain extremely small capture cross-sections

### 4. Current Continuity

**Conclusion:** Current continues to flow solely to maintain neutrality due to the suppressed recombination rate.

## Key Parameters

| Parameter | Symbol | Typical Range |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Capture cross-section | s_n | < 10^-30 cm² |
| Photocarrier lifetime | τ | > 10^6 hours |
| Temperature | T | Low (< 100K) |

## Output
- Theoretical explanation for persistent photoconductivity
- Assessment of barrier formation mechanism
- Prediction of lifetime based on capture cross-section
- Material suitability for PPC applications
