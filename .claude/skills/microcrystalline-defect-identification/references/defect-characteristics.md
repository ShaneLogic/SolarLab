# Microcrystalline Defects Reference

## Microcrystallite Boundaries

### Formation Mechanism
- Result from imperfect growth techniques in glass formation
- Small crystallite nuclei form within the amorphous matrix
- Internal strain of nuclei relaxes through boundary creation

### Structural Characteristics
- Boundaries resemble crystal grain boundaries
- Exhibit localized strain fields
- Act as micro-surfaces between nuclei and glass matrix

### Comparison to Ideal Glass Structure
- Deviation from homogeneous, continuous random network
- Represents partial crystallization tendency
- Can affect electronic and optical properties

## Small Voids

### Location and Formation
- Found specifically in centers of large number rings
- Associated with ring structures that deviate from optimal configurations

### Structural Properties
- Variable size (unlike uniform crystal vacancies)
- Exhibit similarities to lattice vacancies but with key differences:
  - Size is not fixed
  - Bonds may reconfigure around void differently
  - More flexibility in local structure

### Implications
- May influence diffusion pathways
- Can affect material density
- Potential impact on electronic properties

## Material Context

### Affected Materials
- Semiconducting glasses
- Amorphous materials
- Chalcogenide glasses (if applicable to domain)
- Oxide glasses with semiconducting properties

### Detection Methods
- Structural analysis techniques (XRD, TEM, etc.)
- Spectroscopic methods for strain detection
- Computational modeling of ring structures
