# Material Specifications for Solar Cell Device Completion

## Metal Contact Materials

### Adhesion Layer Materials

**Chromium (Cr)**
- Purpose: Adhesion promoter between TCO and aluminum
- Typical thickness: 10-50 nm
- Deposition method: Thermal or e-beam evaporation
- Properties: Good adhesion to oxides, moderate resistivity

**Nickel (Ni)**
- Purpose: Alternative adhesion layer
- Typical thickness: 10-50 nm
- Deposition method: Thermal or e-beam evaporation
- Properties: Good adhesion, lower oxidation tendency than Cr

### Main Conductor Materials

**Aluminum (Al)**
- Primary conductor for front grid
- Typical thickness: ~1 μm
- Deposition method: Thermal evaporation (preferred) or sputtering
- Advantages: Low cost, low resistivity, good conductivity
- Consideration: Forms oxide layer requiring adhesion promoter

## Anti-Reflection Coating Materials

**Magnesium Fluoride (MgF₂)**
- Refractive index: ~1.38
- Optimal thickness: ~100 nm for visible spectrum
- Deposition method: Thermal evaporation
- Purpose: Reduce surface reflection from ~10% to ~3%
- Stability: Excellent environmental stability

## Patterning Considerations

### Grid Design
- Minimize shadow losses while maintaining current collection
- Typical grid coverage: 3-8% of cell area
- Finger width: 50-200 μm depending on deposition method
- Busbar design: Balance between conductivity and shadow loss
