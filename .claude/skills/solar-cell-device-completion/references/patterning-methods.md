# Patterning Methods for Cell Area Definition

## Method A: Mechanical Scribing

**Procedure:**
1. Position scribing tool at cell boundary
2. Apply controlled pressure to remove TCO and absorber layers
3. Stop at Mo back contact
4. Clean debris from scribed area

**Advantages:**
- Simple equipment
- Fast for small batches
- No additional processing steps

**Disadvantages:**
- Less precise than other methods
- Potential for micro-cracks
- Debris generation

## Method B: Laser Patterning

**Procedure:**
1. Program laser path for cell boundary
2. Set laser parameters (wavelength, power, speed)
3. Execute laser scribing to remove layers
4. Verify complete removal at Mo contact

**Advantages:**
- High precision and repeatability
- Suitable for production scale
- Minimal mechanical stress

**Disadvantages:**
- Higher equipment cost
- Requires parameter optimization
- Potential thermal effects

## Method C: Photolithography and Etching

**Procedure:**
1. Apply photoresist over entire surface
2. Expose and develop to define cell area
3. Etch exposed TCO and absorber layers
4. Remove remaining photoresist

**Advantages:**
- Highest precision
- Clean, well-defined edges
- Suitable for complex patterns

**Disadvantages:**
- Multiple processing steps
- Chemical handling required
- Higher cost per cell

## Method D: Masked TCO Deposition

**Procedure:**
1. Fabricate aperture mask with cell area opening
2. Align mask to substrate
3. Deposit TCO through mask opening
4. Remove mask after deposition

**Advantages:**
- Eliminates separate patterning step
- Simple process flow
- Good for research cells

**Disadvantages:**
- Mask alignment critical
- Edge definition less sharp
- Mask wear over multiple uses
