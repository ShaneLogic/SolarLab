# Modulation Doping in Compositional Superlattices

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when:
- Designing doped compositional superlattices or heterostructures
- Optimizing carrier mobility in semiconductor structures
- Analyzing space charge effects in periodic potential systems
- Implementing carrier separation in multi-layer structures

## Prerequisites

- Alternating layers of materials with different band gaps
- Large band-gap material and small band-gap material available

## Procedure

### Step 1: Introduce Dopants
Place donors or acceptors in the large band-gap material only.

### Step 2: Enable Carrier Transfer
Allow free carriers to move into potential wells created by the smaller gap material.

### Step 3: Verify Structural Separation
Confirm separation between:
- Doped material (few carriers remaining)
- Undoped material (substantial carrier density)

### Step 4: Analyze Effects

**Space Charge Creation:**
- Depleted wide-gap layer (if n-type): charged positively
- Lower-gap layer (enriched with electrons): charged negatively

**Band Bending:**
Space charge causes band bending, creating a complicated periodic potential V(x).

**Mobility Enhancement:**
High carrier mobilities achieved within wells due to high-purity undoped material.

## Key Constraints

- Requires pairing of large band-gap and small band-gap materials
- Dopant placement must be confined to wide-gap layers only

## Expected Outcome

Modulation doped superlattice with:
- Spatially separated carriers
- Enhanced mobility in undoped well regions
- Periodic potential profile from band bending
