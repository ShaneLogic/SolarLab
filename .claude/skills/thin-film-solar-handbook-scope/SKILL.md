# Thin-Film Solar Handbook Scope

## When to Use This Skill
Apply this skill when:
- Consulting the Handbook of the Physics of Thin-Film Solar Cells for research
- Planning solar cell design using handbook content
- Evaluating coverage depth for specific thin-film technologies
- Understanding the theoretical framework for CdS-based systems

## Primary Technology Focus
Identify that the handbook provides comprehensive, in-depth coverage with recent references for:
- CdS/Cu2S solar cells
- CdS/CdTe solar cells
- Basic material science principles
- Space charge effects and theoretical analysis

Recognize that these CdS-based systems serve as the primary model for theoretical analysis applicable to most other thin-film solar cells.

## Secondary Technology Coverage
Locate dedicated shorter chapters covering:
- CIS-group: Copper Indium Gallium diSelenide (CIGS)
- Amorphous Silicon (a-Si)

## Handbook Structure
Navigate the content through these organizational layers:
1. History of thin-film solar cell development
2. Recent technologies and advances
3. Commercial viewpoints and applications
4. Global material resources considerations

## Content Exclusions
Understand that the handbook intentionally excludes:
- Detailed descriptions of analytical tools not covered in depth
- Fields of rapidly developing science (to avoid excessive citation lists)

## Author's Perspective
Account for the subjective nature of content selection:
- Based on 40+ years of teaching and research
- Experience-driven toward CdS-related technologies
- Targeted at areas helpful to the author's research

When applying handbook knowledge, recognize that CdS-based systems provide the foundational theoretical framework, with other technologies addressed as extensions of these principles.
