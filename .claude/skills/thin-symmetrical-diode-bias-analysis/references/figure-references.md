## DRO-Range Characteristics

The Domain of Reduced Output (DRO-range) is a critical phenomenon in reverse-biased thin devices:

### Identification Criteria
- Appears when current saturation is approached
- Characterized by strong simultaneous drop of both quasi-Fermi levels
- Visible as slowly sloping, nearly straight segment in n(x) and p(x) profiles

### Physical Significance
- Only minor current increase occurs in this region
- Major bias changes are computed (compare curves 5 and 6)
- Represents the transition to full current saturation

## Diode Quality Factor

### Definition
The diode quality factor A characterizes non-ideal diode behavior:
- A = 1: Ideal diode (diffusion-limited)
- A > 1: Recombination-dominated behavior
- A = 2: Ideal recombination current

### Origin of A > 1 in Thin Devices
Under low forward current (up to ≈15 mA/cm²):
1. go remains larger than r in bulk region
2. Small gr-current of minority carriers flows toward junction
3. This current flows into overshoot region
4. This additional current component causes A > 1

## Current Magnitude Scale

The eight-order-of-magnitude increase from dark to illuminated conditions:
- Dark current: ~10^-10 A/cm²
- Photogenerated current: ~10^-2 A/cm²

This dramatic increase reflects the device's transition from thermally-generated to optically-generated carrier dominance.
