# Edited Volume Chapter Citation Format

## When to Use
Use this skill when formatting bibliographic entries for:
- Individual chapters within edited books
- Contributions to anthologies
- Articles in multi-author volumes

**Trigger indicators:** Entry contains "in" followed by a book title and includes "ed. by" with editor names.

## Required Information
- Chapter author(s)
- Book title (italicized)
- Editor(s)
- Publisher
- Year of publication

## Optional Information
- Volume number
- Chapter pages
- Publisher location

## Formatting Procedure

1. **Identify the Chapter Author(s)**
   - Extract the primary author(s) of the specific chapter
   - Example: "J. D. Wiley"

2. **Locate the Book Title**
   - Find the title following the "in" connector
   - Italicize the title
   - Example: "*Semiconductors and Semimetals*"

3. **Identify Volume Information**
   - Check for volume number (e.g., "vol. 10")
   - Include if present

4. **Extract Editor Information**
   - Find editors via "ed. by" indicator
   - Example: "ed. by R. K. Willardson, A. C. Beer"

5. **Locate Publication Details**
   - Extract publisher name and city
   - Extract year in parentheses
   - Example: "(Academic Press, New York, 1975)"

6. **Check for Page Information**
   - Look for chapter pages at the end
   - May appear as "Chap. 7" or "p. 207"

7. **Construct the Citation**
   ```
   Author. in *Book Title*, vol. X, ed. by Editors. (Publisher, City, Year), pages.
   ```

## Examples

**Basic format:**
```
J. D. Wiley. in *Semiconductors and Semimetals*, vol. 10, ed. by R. K. Willardson, A. C. Beer. (Academic Press, New York, 1975), p. 207.
```

**With chapter reference:**
```
J. D. Wiley. in *Semiconductors and Semimetals*, vol. 10, ed. by R. K. Willardson, A. C. Beer. (Academic Press, New York, 1975), Chap. 7.
```

## Common Patterns
- "in" connector always precedes book title
- "ed. by" indicates editors (not authors)
- Volume appears before editors when present
- Publisher info in parentheses
- Pages at the very end
