# Constant Energy Surface Visualization Examples

## Primitive Cubic Lattice (Fig. 7.7)

### Progressive Filling

1. **Initial state**: Small sphere at zone center
   - Radius: small
   - Shape: nearly perfect sphere
   - Indicates: minimal lattice influence

2. **Intermediate filling**: Larger sphere
   - Radius: increased (parabolic growth)
   - Shape: spherical with slight distortion
   - Indicates: beginning lattice interaction

3. **High filling**: Extended surface
   - Radius: approaching zone boundary
   - Shape: significant distortion
   - Indicates: strong lattice influence

## Binding Potential Evolution (Fig. 7.8)

### 2D Cross-Sectional Views

- **(a) Weak binding**: Nearly circular contours
  - Quasi-free electron behavior
  - Constant energy surfaces approach perfect spheres

- **(b) Moderate binding**: Slightly distorted contours
  - Beginning lattice influence
  - Surfaces deviate from spherical

- **(c) Strong binding**: Clearly distorted contours
  - Significant lattice potential effects
  - Pronounced surface deformation

- **(d) Very strong binding**: Highly distorted contours
  - Dominant lattice influence
  - Complex surface geometry

## Shading Convention

Shaded regions indicate:
- Filled electron states
- Energy levels below reference E₁
- Volume within constant energy surface k₁(E₁)

## Practical Visualization Tips

1. Use 3D rendering for full surface geometry
2. Take 2D slices (cross-sections) for clarity
3. Color-code surfaces by energy level
4. Show progressive filling as animation or series of plots
5. Include Brillouin zone boundaries as reference
