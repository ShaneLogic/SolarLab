# Quantum Mechanical Models and Band Theory Reference

## When to Use
- Analyzing electron states in periodic potentials
- Explaining band structure origins
- Understanding quantum effects in semiconductor devices
- Working with quantum wells, wires, or boxes
- Referencing theoretical foundations of semiconductor physics
- When classical mechanics limits are reached

## Key Categories of Models

### 1. Fundamental Equations and Models
- **Kronig-Penney model**: Periodic potential solutions, band formation
- **Schrödinger equation**: Wave function evolution, energy quantization
- **Golden rule**: Transition probabilities
- **Heisenberg's uncertainty principle**: Position-momentum uncertainty
- **Pauli principle**: Fermion exclusion

### 2. Quantum Structures and States
- **Quantum boxes**: Zero-dimensional confinement
- **Quantum well structures**: Two-dimensional confinement
- **Quantum wires**: One-dimensional confinement
- **Quasi-free electrons**: Nearly-free electron approximation
- **Quasi-particles**: Composite excitations (e.g., excitons, polarons)

### 3. Band Structure Concepts
- **Mini-bands**: Superlattice formation, narrow bands from quantum coupling
- **Heavy/light hole bands**: Valence band structure from degeneracy lifting
- **Indirect band-gap materials**: Momentum-change transitions required
- **Graded band-gap**: Spatially varying bandgap for built-in fields
- **Interspersed forbidden gaps**: Additional gaps in complex structures
- **Lifshitz tail**: Density of states tail from disorder

### 4. Fermi Level and Statistics
- **Quasi-Fermi level**: Non-equilibrium carrier statistics
- **Quasi-neutrality condition**: Approximation for charge balance

## Reference Usage Guide

This skill provides indexed access to quantum theory concepts. For detailed derivations or specific equation applications, consult the references section.

## When Classical Physics Fails

Apply quantum models when:
- Particle-wave duality is significant
- Energy quantization affects device behavior
- Tunneling or barrier penetration occurs
- Atomic-scale dimensions are involved
- Discrete energy levels are observable
- Periodic potentials create band structure
