# Detailed Mechanisms of Carrier-Induced Plastic Effects

## Initiation Mechanisms

### Carrier Injection Pathways

1. **Electrical Injection (Electroplastic)**
   - Direct electrical current applied
   - Carriers injected from contacts
   - Common in device operation scenarios

2. **Optical Generation (Photoplastic)**
   - Photon absorption creates electron-hole pairs
   - Requires bandgap-appropriate photon energy
   - Can be focused or uniform illumination

3. **Cathode-Related Injection (Cathodoplastic)**
   - Electron beam irradiation
   - Cathode ray exposure
   - High-energy electron bombardment

## Dislocation Charge State Dynamics

### Charge Density Changes

```
Δλ = q × (n_injected - n_recombined)
```

Where:
- λ = charge per unit length of dislocation
- q = elementary charge
- n_injected = carrier concentration at dislocation
- n_recombined = recombined carrier concentration

### Recharging of Defect Centers

Defect centers can act as:
- Electron traps
- Hole traps
- Recombination centers

The recharging affects both:
1. Local electric fields
2. Dislocation mobility

## Dislocation Climb Mechanics

### Normal Climb Process

Dislocation climb occurs by:
- Absorption/emission of point defects (vacancies or interstitials)
- Movement perpendicular to slip plane
- Requires diffusion at finite temperature

### Carrier-Induced Modification

Carrier density affects climb through:
1. **Electrostatic interaction** between charged dislocation and point defects
2. **Modified diffusion barriers** due to local charge state
3. **Recombination-enhanced defect motion** (REDR effect)

## Material-Specific Behavior

### II-VI Compound Semiconductors

**Why effects are pronounced:**
- High ionic character of bonding
- Strong electron-phonon coupling
- Multiple native defect types
- Often exhibit deep levels acting as efficient traps

**Common II-VI compounds:**
- ZnS, ZnSe, ZnTe
- CdS, CdSe, CdTe
- HgS, HgSe, HgTe

## Photoquenching Exception

### Mechanism

Photoquenching occurs when:
- Specific defect centers are optically ionized
- This changes their charge state in a way that facilitates dislocation motion
- Results in softening (decreased mechanical strength)

### Conditions for Photoquenching

- Requires specific defect energy levels
- Photon energy must match defect ionization energy
- Often observed at low temperatures
- Depends on initial defect charge state

## Quantitative Considerations

### Typical Effect Magnitudes

Material-dependent examples:
- CdS: up to 50% change in yield stress under illumination
- ZnSe: significant flow stress reduction under electron beam
- GaAs: moderate effects (less pronounced than II-VI compounds)

### Time Scales

- Fast response: nanoseconds to microseconds (carrier lifetime)
- Slow response: seconds to minutes (defect reconfiguration)
- Persistent effects: hours to days (metastable configurations)

## Experimental Considerations

### Measurement Techniques

1. **Mechanical Testing**
   - Microhardness indentation
   - Nanoindentation
   - Compression/tension tests under illumination

2. **Characterization**
   - Dislocation etch pit density
   - TEM dislocation imaging
   - Photoluminescence spectroscopy

### Control Variables

- Temperature
- Carrier injection rate
- Light intensity/wavelength
- Dislocation density
- Impurity concentration

## Theoretical Frameworks

### Rehbinder Effect (Historical Context)

Surface-active agents reducing strength - related but distinct from carrier-induced effects.

### Electron-Dislocation Interaction Models

1. **Cottrell atmosphere model** - modified for electronic effects
2. **Suzuki segregation** - electronic analog
3. **Electrostatic pinning** - charged dislocations interacting with defects

## Applications

### Material Processing
- Controlled strengthening/weakening of semiconductors
- Stress relaxation in devices
- Fatigue life modification

### Device Engineering
- Radiation hardening strategies
- Optomechanical devices
- Smart material behavior

## Safety and Practical Notes

- High carrier injection can cause Joule heating
- Electron beam exposure can introduce radiation damage
- Photoquenching may be irreversible in some materials
