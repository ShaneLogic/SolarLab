# Hydrogen-Acceptor Passivation in Silicon

## When to Use
- Hydrogen is present in silicon with acceptors
- Analyzing dramatic reduction in p-type conductivity
- Calculating dissociation energies for H-acceptor pairs
- Studying hydrogen diffusion at moderate temperatures

## Analyze Hydrogen Diffusion

1. **Determine Diffusion Conditions**
   - Hydrogen diffuses as an interstitial
   - Temperature threshold: ≤ 400°C
   - Moderate temperatures required for significant diffusion

2. **Assess Conductivity Impact**
   - Dramatically reduces p-type conductivity
   - Has almost no effect on n-type conductivity

## Analyze Pair Formation

1. **Identify Formation Mechanism**
   - Spatial distribution tracks that of acceptors
   - Forms electrically neutral Hydrogen-Acceptor pair via compensation and Coulomb attraction
   - Reaction: (A⁻) + (H⁺) → (A-H)⁰

2. **Calculate Dissociation Energy**
   - For Boron-Hydrogen (B-H): Estimated at 0.59 eV
   - Reaction: (BH)x → B⁻ + Hx (Hx at BC site)
   - Note: This value may be too low to explain reactivation at ~150°C

3. **Determine Site Geometry**
   - **BM Site**: Lies at ~43% off B, between B and Si in the (110) plane
   - **BC Site**: Lies halfway between Si atoms in an undisturbed lattice

## Output
- Formation of neutral H-Acceptor pair
- Modification of conductivity characteristics
- Dissociation energy values
- Site geometry information
