# Deep Level Center Examples and Edge Cases

## Example 1: Transition Metal Impurity in Silicon

- Iron in silicon creates deep levels in the band gap
- Deep levels at approximately 0.4 eV and 0.7 eV from band edges
- Higher excited states show hydrogenic Rydberg series

## Example 2: Vacancy in Diamond

- Neutral vacancy creates deep levels near mid-gap
- Strongly localized wavefunction on neighboring carbon atoms
- Short-range potential dominates due to lattice distortion

## Edge Cases

### Borderline Deep-Shallow Defects

When core potential is approximately 5-10 eV:
- Ground state may exhibit partial delocalization
- Mixing between deep and shallow characteristics
- Requires more sophisticated models (e.g., tight-binding, DFT)

### High Dielectric Constant Materials

- Screening reduces Coulomb tail influence
- More excited states remain deep-like
- Effective mass approximation fails more completely

### Strained Systems

- Pressure/strain can shift energy levels
- Deep centers show smaller shifts compared to shallow centers
- Can be used experimentally to distinguish deep from shallow levels

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Using effective mass**: Deep centers use electron rest mass, not effective mass
2. **Ignoring Coulomb tail**: Even deep centers have hydrogen-like excited states if charged
3. **Assuming parabolic bands**: Deep levels connect across the entire Brillouin zone
4. **Single-band models**: Deep centers involve both conduction and valence band coupling
