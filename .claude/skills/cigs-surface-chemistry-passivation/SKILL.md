# CIGS Surface Chemistry and Passivation

## When to Use
- Analyzing surface composition changes in Cu(InGa)Se2 films
- Optimizing solar cell performance through passivation
- Investigating Cu migration mechanisms
- Evaluating film stability and oxidation effects

## Surface Composition Changes and Cu Migration

### Composition Gradient Formation
- Composition changes gradually from bulk to surface
- Caused by Cu migration driven by surface conditions

### Surface Fermi Level Effects
- Surface Fermi level approaches conduction band due to:
  - Surface defects
  - Adsorbates
  - Junction formation

### Cu Depletion Mechanism
- **Interpretation:** Reduced Cu concentration = formation of Cu vacancies
- **Driving force:** Cu vacancy formation becomes more favorable with higher Fermi level

### Band Bending Effects
- Band bending induced by surface charges
- Drives electromigrating Cu into bulk
- Leaves surface depleted of Cu

### Depletion Limit
- **Stopping point:** Cu depletion stops at CuIn3Se5 composition
- **Further depletion:** Requires structural change of material

### Type Conversion Correlation
- Electro-migration of Cu correlates with type conversion of chalcopyrite

### Oxidation Effects
- **Copper diffusion:** Reduced when oxides form on surface
- **Surface oxidation:** Enhanced by Na
- **Identified compounds:** In2O3, Ga2O3, SeOx, Na2CO3

## Oxygen and Sodium Passivation

### Post-Treatment Procedure
- **Temperature:** Typically 200°C
- **Atmosphere:** Air
- **Timing:** After CdS or (CdZn)S vacuum deposition
- **Duration:** Extended for several hours for optimization

### Oxygen Passivation Mechanism

#### Target
- Selenium vacancies (V_Se) on surface of grains

#### Problem
- Donor-type selenium vacancy acts as recombination center
- Positive charge reduces effective hole concentration
- Impedes inter-grain carrier transport

#### Solution
- Oxygen substitutes missing selenium
- Cancels impediment to carrier transport

### Sodium Effects

#### Beneficial Effects
- Performance improvement mechanism not fully understood

#### Possible Mechanisms
1. **Catalytic effect on oxidation:**
   - Enhanced dissociation of molecular oxygen into atomic oxygen
   - Makes passivation of V_Se more effective

2. **Location:**
   - Na and O predominantly at grain boundaries
   - Little composition difference between interior and boundary

### Grain Boundary Insensitivity

#### Observations
- Cu(InGa)Se2 solar cells show insensitivity to grain size and morphology
- Indicates no significant recombination loss at grain boundaries
- Polycrystalline Cu(InGa)Te2 cells outperform single-crystalline cells

#### Implications
- Grain boundaries may be beneficial for solar cells
- Supports tolerance to small grain sizes

## Critical Insights
- **Limiting composition:** CuIn3Se5
- **Key mechanism:** Oxygen substitution at selenium vacancy sites
- **Surface stability:** Dependent on oxidation and Cu migration balance
