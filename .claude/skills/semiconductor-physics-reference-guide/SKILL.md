# Semiconductor Physics Reference Guide

Use this skill when conducting literature reviews, seeking foundational references, or identifying seminal works in specific semiconductor physics subdomains.

## When to Use
- Researching amorphous semiconductors and switching phenomena
- Studying optical properties and spectroscopy techniques
- Investigating semiconductor defects and crystal growth
- Working on Schottky barrier junctions and metal-semiconductor interfaces
- Building reference collections for specific research topics

## Reference Navigation

### 1. Identify Research Subdomain
Determine which of the following areas matches your research focus:
- Amorphous semiconductors and switching phenomena
- Optical properties and spectroscopy
- Defects, crystal growth, and fundamental theory
- Schottky barrier junctions

### 2. Consult Reference Lists
Access the curated reference lists for the identified subdomain from the references section.

### 3. Evaluate Reference Relevance
Review citations for:
- Publication date (foundational vs. current)
- Author reputation in the field
- Relevance to specific research question
- Citation frequency and impact

### 4. Expand Search
Use identified key references to find related works through:
- Forward citation tracking
- Author publication history
- Similar topic keywords

## Domain Coverage

This skill covers four main semiconductor physics subdomains:

**Amorphous Semiconductors**: Ovshinsky effect, switching phenomena, structural excitations
**Optical Properties**: Optical constants, dielectric functions, spectroscopic methods
**Defects & Growth**: Deep centers, crystal growth theory, fundamental solid state physics
**Schottky Barriers**: Metal-semiconductor interfaces, barrier theory, device applications
