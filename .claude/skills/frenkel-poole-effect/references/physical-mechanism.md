# Physical Mechanism Details

## Coulomb Potential with External Field

### Potential Distribution
The total potential at distance r from a charged center:
```
V(r) = -Ze/(4πεε₀r) - F×r×cos(θ)
```

### Barrier Maximum
The barrier maximum shifts from the origin:
- Position: r_max = √(Ze/4πεε₀F)
- Height reduction: δE = √(Ze³F/πεε₀)

## Comparison with Other Ionization Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Field Requirement | Process |
|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| Frenkel-Poole | ~10 kV/cm | Barrier lowering + thermal |
| Impact Ionization | ~100 kV/cm | Carrier multiplication |
| Tunneling | ~1000 kV/cm | Direct tunneling |

## Graphical Representation

### Potential Funnel
Without field: Symmetric Coulomb potential
With field: Funnel tilted in field direction
- Barrier lowered on one side
- Barrier raised on opposite side
- Net effect: Enhanced emission

## Material-Specific Examples

### CdS:Cu System
- Copper creates deep acceptor levels
- Acts as hole trap when ionized
- Field releases trapped holes
- Affects photoconductive gain

### Other Materials
| Material | Defect | Application |

## Temperature Dependence

The Frenkel-Poole effect is a thermally-assisted process:
- Lower temperatures: Less effective
- Higher temperatures: Enhanced emission
- Field reduces activation energy

## Limitations

1. Only applies to Coulomb-attractive centers
2. Neutral or repulsive centers: No effect
3. Very high fields: Tunneling dominates
4. Very low temperatures: Thermal emission suppressed
