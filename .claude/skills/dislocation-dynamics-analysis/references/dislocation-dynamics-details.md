# Dislocation Dynamics Reference Details

## Velocity in Semiconductors
Dislocation velocity is significantly influenced by the electrical properties of the material:

**Example: Germanium (Ge)**
- Velocity in n-type Ge is approximately 50 times larger than in p-type Ge under identical conditions
- This difference is due to the Fermi level position affecting the electrical charge of defects

## Frank-Read Source Mechanism
The Frank-Read source is a primary mechanism for dislocation generation:

1. A dislocation segment is pinned at two points (e.g., by impurities or other dislocations)
2. Under applied shear stress, the segment bows outward
3. As stress increases, the bowing continues until the segment forms a loop
4. The loop closes and detaches, leaving the original pinned segment
5. The process repeats periodically, generating multiple loops

## Kink Mechanics
Double kinks play a crucial role in dislocation motion:

1. **Nucleation**: A pair of kinks (double kink) nucleates along the dislocation line
2. **Propagation**: The kinks move in opposite directions along the line
3. **Result**: The dislocation advances by one atomic spacing

The velocity of the dislocation depends on both the nucleation rate of double kinks and their subsequent motion along the line.
