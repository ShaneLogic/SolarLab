# Chalcogen Impurities in Silicon

## When to Use
- Calculating energy levels for chalcogen (O, S, Se, Te) impurities in silicon
- Analyzing ground state splitting (A1, E, T2) of deep donors
- Investigating chalcogen pair formation and symmetry properties
- Studying pressure coefficients of deep donor states

## Analyze Single Chalcogen Donors

1. **Identify Donor Type**
   - Chalcogens (O, S, Se, Te) act as deep donors
   - Often form associates (especially Oxygen) or act as double donors

2. **Analyze Ground States (Deep)**
   - The 1s ground state splits due to crystal field into three components:
     - **A1**: Non-degenerate (Deepest)
     - **E**: Doubly degenerate
     - **T2**: Threefold degenerate
   - Use binding energies from reference data (Table 10.1)

3. **Analyze Excited States (Shallow)**
   - p-states are shallow and follow effective mass (hydrogen-like) approximation
   - Example: 2p0 ~11.5 meV (close to E_Hydrogen ~11.75 meV)

4. **Pressure Coefficient Analysis**
   - Compare hydrostatic pressure coefficient of ground state (1s) to hydrogen-like centers
   - Larger coefficient (~ -1.7 to -1.8 x 10^-8 eV/Pa for S/Se) indicates connection to conduction bands (X-valley)

## Analyze Chalcogen Pairs

1. **Determine Center Nature**
   - Molecular pairs of S, Se, or Te act as single, deep centers
   - Depth: Slightly lower than single chalcogen substitutionals
   - Excited states: Very close to corresponding hydrogen-like states

2. **Identify Structure and Symmetry**
   - Incorporation: (111) axial symmetry
   - Ground states: Four distinct 1s ground states (A1g, Eg, Eu, A1u)

3. **Assess Anisotropy**
   - Any pair is anisotropic, even in isotropic crystal
   - Orientation examples: [100], [010], or [001] for nearest-neighbor pairs
   - Optical excitation is polarization-dependent

4. **Consider Mixed Pairs**
   - Mixed pairs are observed (e.g., Si:S;Se or Si:Se;Te)

## Output
- Specific binding energies for A1, E, T2 states
- Characterization of deep center state and symmetry
- Identification of shallow vs deep characteristics
