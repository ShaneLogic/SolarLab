# Single Crystal Growth Techniques

## When to Use
- Producing single crystal semiconductors for research or device fabrication
- Selecting growth method based on material properties and requirements
- Optimizing crystal quality and size

## Technique Selection Guide

### Vapor Phase Growth
**Use when:** Material has suitable vapor pressure, high-purity crystals needed

**Methods:**
1. **Evaporation**
   - In vacuo, inert atmosphere, or reacting atmosphere
   - Reacting atmosphere maintains stoichiometry of dissociating compounds

2. **Spontaneous Nucleation**
   - Sublimation method (e.g., CdS forming platelets)

3. **Seeding**
   - Controlled nucleation using seed crystal

4. **Epitaxy**
   - Vapor phase deposition onto identical/similar lattice
   - **Molecular Beam Epitaxy (MBE):** Atomic/molecular beams in vacuo
   - Dissociation of evaporated compound (e.g., organometallic)

### Melt Growth
**Use when:** Material melts congruently, large crystals needed

**Methods:**
1. **Czochralski Method**
   - Dip cooled seed from top surface
   - Slow pulling to grow crystal
   - **Limiting factor:** Heat removal through grown crystal

2. **Bridgeman Technique**
   - Utilizes anisotropic growth rate (e.g., largest in c-direction)
   - Random crystallization in narrow tip at bottom
   - Aligned crystals outgrow others and act as oriented seed

### Solution Growth
**Use when:** Material decomposes before melting, low-temperature growth needed

**Solvents:** Water, organic solvents, liquid metals, molten solids

**Initiation:** Evaporate solvent or lower temperature to achieve supersaturation

**Note:** Liquid metal/molten solid solvents show phase segregation before solidification

### Chemical Reaction Growth
**Use when:** Direct synthesis from precursors needed

**Requirements:** Reaction must proceed slowly to avoid by-product incorporation

**Methods:**
1. **Spray Deposition** - Highly diluted reaction partners onto heated plate
2. **Gels** - Contain reaction partners, permit slow interdiffusion
