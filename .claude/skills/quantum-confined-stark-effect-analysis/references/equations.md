# Equations for Quantum Confined Stark Effect

## Ionization Condition

When electric field is normal to layers, ionization occurs when:

```
e * F * a_B > E_b
```

Where:
- `e` = elementary charge (1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ C)
- `F` = applied electric field (V/cm or V/m)
- `a_B` = Bohr radius of the confined state
- `E_b` = binding energy of the defect or exciton

## Stark Shift Formulas

### Quadratic Stark Regime (Lower Fields)

The change in binding energy follows:

```
ΔE_b ∝ F² * l⁴
```

This is the quadratic (second-order) Stark effect, dominant at lower field strengths.

### Linear Stark Regime (Higher Fields)

The change in binding energy follows:

```
ΔE_b ∝ F * l³
```

This represents the linear (first-order) Stark effect, which becomes significant at higher field strengths.

## Reference Equations

## Typical Field Magnitudes

- **Parallel field ionization**: ~10 kV/cm
- **Normal field ionization threshold**: >10^5 V/cm (100 kV/cm)

The significantly higher tolerance for normal fields is due to quantum confinement in the well structure.
