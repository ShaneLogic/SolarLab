# Phase Transition Details

## Protocrystalline Regime
- Characterized by medium-range order
- Optimal for stable a-Si:H solar cells
- Typically achieved with moderate to high H2 dilution

## Mixed Phase Characteristics
- Coexistence of amorphous and crystalline phases
- Critical for understanding transition dynamics
- Can be identified through Raman spectroscopy

## Nanocrystalline Silicon Properties
- Grain size typically 3-10 nm
- Higher carrier mobility than a-Si:H
- Better stability under prolonged illumination
- Lower absorption coefficient requires thicker layers

## Process Optimization
- H2 dilution ratio is the primary control parameter
- Substrate temperature also influences phase formation
- Deposition pressure affects film morphology
- RF power density impacts crystallite nucleation
