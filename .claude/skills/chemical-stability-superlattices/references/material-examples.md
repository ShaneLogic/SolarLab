# Semiconductor Superlattice Material Examples

## Common Isovalent Systems
- **GaAs-AlAs**: Classic III-V semiconductor superlattice
- **Behavior**: Forms Ga1-xAlxAs alloy
- **Stability**: Unstable to segregation, frozen at room temperature

## Large Mismatch Systems
- **Si-Ge**: Group IV semiconductor pairing
- **GaAs-InAs**: III-V systems with significant lattice mismatch
- **Behavior**: More stable than ultrathin superlattices
- **Energy**: Higher alloy formation energy than ultrathin systems

## Spontaneous Ordering Examples
- **(GaAs)1(AlAs)1**: Forms spontaneously near 840K
- **Significance**: Demonstrates natural ordering without artificial deposition
- **Implication**: Some systems favor ordered compound states under thermal conditions

## Stability Comparison Summary
| System Type | Stability | Mechanism | Key Feature |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Isovalent | Unstable | Alloy formation | No nucleation required |
| Low Mismatch | Unstable | Alloy formation | Thermodynamically driven |
| Large Mismatch | More Stable | Higher energy barrier | Relative to ultrathin layers |

## Temperature Thresholds
- **Room temperature**: Recrystallization frozen for most systems
- **840K**: Spontaneous ordering threshold for (GaAs)1(AlAs)1
- **General trend**: Higher temperatures accelerate alloy formation kinetics
