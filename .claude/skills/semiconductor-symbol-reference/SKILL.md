# Semiconductor Symbol Reference

Identify the meaning of physics symbols based on context, case, and notation.

## When to Use

- Encountering unfamiliar symbols in semiconductor physics equations
- Distinguishing between vector (bold) and scalar quantities
- Interpreting energy level notation in band diagrams
- Identifying distribution functions in statistical mechanics
- Decoding field vectors in electromagnetic problems

## Quick Reference

| Symbol | Primary Meanings |
|--------|------------------|
| **E** | Electric field vector, Energy |
| **e** | Elementary charge, Electron, Unit vector |
| **F** | Electric field vector, Helmholtz free energy, Force |
| **f** | Distribution function, Fermi function |
| **G** | Gibbs free energy, Density of states, Generation rate |
| **H** | Hamiltonian, Magnetic field vector, Enthalpy |
| **h** | Planck's constant, Hole |

## Interpretation Rules

### Case Sensitivity
- **Uppercase** (E, F, G, H): Typically macroscopic quantities, total energies, or field vectors
- **Lowercase** (e, f, g, h): Typically microscopic quantities, particles, or rate functions

### Bold Notation
- **Bold symbols** indicate vectors (e.g., **E** = electric field vector)
- Regular typeface indicates scalars

### Subscript Context
Subscripts modify meaning based on physical context:
- Energy subscripts (c, v, g, F): Band structure references
- Particle subscripts (n, p): Carrier-specific quantities
- Process subscripts (ion, diss, subl): Thermodynamic processes

## Common Symbol Groups

### Energy-Related (E)
- EF: Fermi energy
- Ec, Ev: Conduction/valence band edges
- Eg: Bandgap
- Ed, Ea, Et: Donor, acceptor, trap energies
- Ea: Activation energy

### Field-Related (F)
- Fi: Built-in (internal) field
- Fe: External field
- Fl: Local field

### Distribution Functions (f)
- fFD: Fermi-Dirac distribution
- fBE: Bose-Einstein distribution
- fB: Boltzmann distribution
- fn, fp: Fermi functions for electrons/holes

### Generation/States (G, g)
- G: Gibbs free energy, Number of states
- g: Density of states, Generation rate
- go: Optical generation rate

Return the specific meaning of the symbol based on:
