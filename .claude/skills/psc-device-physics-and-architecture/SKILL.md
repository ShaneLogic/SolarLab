# PSC Device Physics and Architecture

Use this skill when analyzing the physical structure and fundamental charge generation mechanisms of planar Perovskite Solar Cells (PSCs), or when working with metal halide perovskite materials (e.g., CH3NH3PbI3).

## Identify the Layered Structure

1. **Electron Transport Layer (ETL)**
   - Common material: Titania (TiO2)
   - Located at the interface where electrons are collected

2. **Perovskite Absorber Layer**
   - Common material: Methylammonium lead tri-iodide (CH3NH3PbI3)
   - Where photon absorption occurs

3. **Hole Transport Layer (HTL)**
   - Common material: spiro-MeOTAD
   - Located at the interface where holes are collected

## Understand Photon Absorption and Exciton Dissociation

1. **Photon Absorption**
   - Incident photons with energy above the band gap are absorbed in the perovskite layer

2. **Exciton Generation**
   - Weakly bound excitons are generated with binding energy ~50 meV

3. **Exciton Dissociation**
   - Excitons dissociate into free electrons (conduction band) and holes (valence band)

## Analyze Charge Transport

1. **Carrier Motion Mechanisms**
   - Carriers move under thermally-induced diffusion
   - Carriers move under electronically-induced drift

2. **Selective Carrier Extraction**
   - Energy barriers prevent holes from entering the ETL
   - Energy barriers prevent electrons from entering the HTL

3. **Built-in Field Effects**
   - Built-in field drives electrons towards the ETL
   - Built-in field drives holes towards the HTL

## Key Variables

- **ETL**: Material (e.g., TiO2)
- **HTL**: Material (e.g., spiro-MeOTAD)
- **Band Gap**: Energy threshold for photon absorption

## Output

A complete description of the device stack and the flow of electrons and holes from generation to collection.
