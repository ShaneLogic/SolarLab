# Intercalated Compound Synthesis Reference

## Temperature Guidelines
| Intercalant | Temperature Range |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Lithium (Li) | 200–400°C |

## Example Compounds and Properties

### LiC6 (Lithium-Graphite Intercalation Compound)
- Appearance: Yellow, transparent
- Conductivity: Anisotropic (direction-dependent)
- Superconductivity: Exhibits low-temperature superconductivity

### KC8 (Potassium-Graphite Intercalation Compound)
- Similar anisotropic properties
- Used in battery applications

## Chemical Mechanism Details
The intercalation process involves:
1. Metal atoms ionize (M → M⁺ + e⁻)
2. Electrons transfer to host crystal layers
3. Host layers become negatively charged
4. Interlayer spacing increases to accommodate intercalant ions

## Battery Technology Applications
- Primary mechanism in lithium-ion batteries
- Enables reversible charge/discharge cycles
- High energy density due to efficient intercalation
- Graphite anodes in commercial batteries use LiC₆ formation

## Diffusion Kinetics
- Intercalation initiates at crystal edges
- Rapid diffusion into bulk material
- Diffusion rate depends on:
  - Temperature
  - Intercalant size
  - Host crystal defect density
  - Interlayer spacing
