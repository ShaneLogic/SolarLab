# Semiconductor Physics Literature Retrieval

Use this skill when searching for scientific references in semiconductor research, device physics, and related fields.

## When to Use

- Writing literature reviews for semiconductor or solid state physics
- Finding foundational works on semiconductor devices (LEDs, tunneling)
- Searching for material characterization techniques (STM, AFM, spectroscopy)
- Locating references for amorphous semiconductors and insulators
- Researching surface science and analytical methods
- Investigating electron tunneling, transport properties, or spin physics

## Key Research Domains

- **Semiconductor Devices**: LEDs, diodes, optoelectronic components
- **Solid State Theory**: Tunneling, transport, spin physics
- **Material Characterization**: STM, AFM, microscopy techniques
- **Surface Science**: Spectroscopy, surface analysis, adsorption
- **Amorphous Materials**: Theory, properties, and applications

## Retrieval Procedure

1. Identify the specific research domain or technique
2. Consult the domain-specific reference lists in `references/semiconductor-physics-citations.md`
3. Select relevant citations based on:
   - Foundational vs. applied nature of the work
   - Specific techniques or phenomena of interest
   - Historical context (seminal papers vs. recent advances)
4. Verify publication details before using in bibliography

## Notable Authors and Works

- **LEDs**: A.A. Bergh, P.J. Dean - Early LED development and comprehensive texts
- **Microscopy**: G. Binnig, H. Rohrer - Nobel-winning work on STM and AFM
- **Semiconductor Statistics**: J.S. Blakemore, R.B. Bird et al. - Transport theory foundations
- **Amorphous Materials**: D.A. Drabold, S.R. Elliott, D. Emin - Theory and properties
- **Tunneling**: C.B. Duke, L. Esaki - Fundamental treatises and discoveries
- **Surface Science**: L.H. Dubois, G.A. Somorjai, R.M. Feenstra - Surface analysis techniques
