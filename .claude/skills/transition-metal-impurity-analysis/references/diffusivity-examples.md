# Transition Metal Diffusivity Reference Data

## Nickel (Ni) in Silicon (Si)

### Configuration Behavior
- Ni achieves noble metal configuration most effectively among transition metals in Si
- This results in extremely high diffusivity

### Diffusivity Value
- **D = 10^-4 cm²/s**
- This value indicates:
  - Small effective atomic size
  - Inert chemical behavior
  - Rapid diffusion through Si lattice

### Physical Interpretation
The high diffusivity of Ni in Si is direct evidence that:
1. The Ni atom has achieved a stable, noble-metal-like configuration
2. The atom presents minimal interaction with the host lattice
3. The effective atomic radius is reduced due to electron configuration

## Compensation Applications

### Mechanism
Transition metals with noble metal configurations can:
- Trap charge carriers (electrons or holes)
- Reduce free carrier concentration
- Decrease semiconductor conductivity

### Use Cases
- Producing semi-insulating silicon
- Controlling leakage currents in devices
- Creating high-resistance regions in integrated circuits

### Selection Criteria
Choose transition metals with:
- High diffusivity (for uniform distribution)
- Appropriate energy levels for carrier trapping
- Minimal interference with other dopants
