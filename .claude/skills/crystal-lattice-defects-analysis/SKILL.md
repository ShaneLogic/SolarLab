# Crystal Lattice Defects Analysis

## When to Use This Skill
Apply this skill when you need to:
- Identify types of crystal lattice defects in crystalline materials
- Classify grain boundaries and their characteristics
- Analyze dislocation types and their motion mechanisms
- Determine lattice imperfections and their structural impact
- Characterize point defects in semiconductor materials

## Classification Framework

### 1. Grain Boundaries
Classify boundary types based on misorientation angles and structural characteristics:
- **General grain boundaries**: High-angle boundaries between crystallites
- **Low-angle grain boundaries**: Small misorientation angles, often composed of discrete dislocations
- **Inter-grain barriers**: Obstacles at grain boundaries affecting transport properties
- **Hetero-boundaries**: Interfaces between different materials or crystal structures

### 2. Dislocations and Motion
Identify dislocation types and their movement characteristics:
- **Glide motion**: Dislocation movement along specific crystallographic planes
- **Glide plane**: The plane along which dislocations preferentially move
- **Glide set**: Family of slip systems enabling dislocation motion
- **Screw dislocation**: Dislocation line parallel to Burgers vector
- **Mixed dislocation**: Combination of edge and screw character
- **Partial dislocations**: Dislocations with fractional Burgers vectors
- **Sessile**: Immobile dislocations that cannot glide
- **Shuffle set**: Alternative slip system with different displacement vectors
- **Network of dislocations**: Interconnected dislocation structures
- **Pinning of dislocations**: Mechanisms that restrict dislocation motion

### 3. Lattice Imperfections
Characterize defects that disrupt crystal periodicity:
- **Lattice mismatch**: Difference in lattice constants between materials
- **Mismatch dislocation**: Dislocations formed to accommodate lattice mismatch
- **Missing lattice atom**: Vacancies in the crystal structure
- **Missing charges**: Charge imbalances due to defects
- **Large lattice defects**: Extended defects affecting multiple lattice sites
- **Point defects**: Zero-dimensional defects (vacancies, interstitials, substitutionals)

## Analysis Procedure

1. **Examine the crystal structure** for deviations from perfect periodicity
2. **Identify the scale of the defect** (point, line, plane, volume)
3. **Classify the defect type** using the framework above
4. **Determine the impact** on material properties (mechanical, electrical, optical)
5. **Note any relationships** between defects (e.g., dislocation networks, grain boundary arrays)
