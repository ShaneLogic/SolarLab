# Detailed Solar Economics Calculations

## Panel Cost Comparison

### Thin-Film Panels
- Cost: ~$2 per peak Watt
- Lower efficiency requires more surface area
- Newer technology with <10 years large-scale market history

### Crystalline Silicon Panels
- Cost: ~$4 per peak Watt
- Higher efficiency requires less surface area
- 40+ years market history
- More established technology

## Installation Cost Breakdown

| Component | Cost Range |
|-----------|------------|
| Thin-film panels | $2/Watt |
| Crystalline silicon panels | $4/Watt |
| Installation labor | $2-$5/Watt |
| Total installed cost | $7,000-$9,000/kW |

## Tax Incentive Examples

### Federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC)
- 30% of total system cost
- Applies to both residential and commercial systems

### State-Level Incentives
- Varies significantly by state
- Can include additional tax credits, rebates, or performance-based incentives
- Combined federal + state typically reduces cost by 30-50%

## Payback Period Calculation Formula

```
Annual Savings = System Size (kW) × Sunshine Hours/Day × 365 × Utility Rate ($/kWh)

Payback Period (years) = Total Cost After Incentives / Annual Savings
```

### Example Calculation
- System size: 3 kW
- Sunshine hours: 3 hours/day
- Utility rate: $0.10/kWh
- Total cost after incentives: $15,000

Annual Savings = 3 × 3 × 365 × 0.10 = $328.50/year
Payback Period = 15,000 / 328.50 = 45.7 years

Note: Actual savings depend on system size, local utility rates, and sunshine hours.

## Market Context
- Silicon panels have 40+ years market history
- Large-scale CdS/CdTe (thin-film) has <10 years market history
- Installation costs vary significantly by region and installer
- Tax incentives are subject to policy changes
