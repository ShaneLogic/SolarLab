# Ionicity and Bonding Examples

## CdS Bonding Analysis

**Purely ionic state:** Cd²⁺ + S²⁻
- Complete charge transfer
- e* = 1.00 (theoretical maximum)

**Purely covalent state:** Cd²⁻ + S²⁺
- Equal sharing of electrons
- e* = 0.00

**Actual measured state:** e* = 0.49
- Indicates ~49% ionic character
- Mixed bonding between ionic and covalent extremes

## Interpreting Effective Charge Values

### e* < 0.3
- Predominantly covalent bonding
- Similar to Group IV elements
- Example: Si, Ge

### 0.3 ≤ e* ≤ 0.6
- Mixed ionic-covalent bonding
- Typical of most compound semiconductors
- Example: GaAs (0.46), ZnS (0.47)

### e* > 0.6
- Predominantly ionic bonding
- Approaches ionic insulator behavior
- Example: ZnO (0.60)

## Trend Across Semiconductor Groups

| Group | Typical e* Range | Bonding Character |
|-------|------------------|-------------------|
| Group IV | 0.00 - 0.05 | Purely covalent |
| III-V | 0.40 - 0.55 | Mixed, moderate ionicity |
| II-VI | 0.45 - 0.70 | Mixed, high ionicity |

The trend shows increasing ionicity as the electronegativity difference between constituent atoms increases.
