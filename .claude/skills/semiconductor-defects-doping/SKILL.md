# Semiconductor Defects and Doping

Analyze defect structures and impurity behavior in semiconductor materials.

## When to Use

- Analyzing hydrogen incorporation in amorphous silicon (α-Si:H)
- Understanding donor impurity behavior in crystalline semiconductors
- Determining defect passivation mechanisms
- Predicting energy levels from dopant atoms
- Analyzing coordination changes in amorphous networks

## Part 1: Hydrogen in Amorphous Silicon

### Analysis Approach

When analyzing hydrogen incorporation into α-Si:H:

1. **Use atomic clusters** derived from the network structure
2. **Include multiple neighbor shells**:
   - Nearest neighbors
   - Next-nearest neighbors
   - Higher-order neighbors from the alloyed atom

### Incorporation Mechanism

Hydrogen incorporation proceeds by **decorating defects with hydrogen**:

1. Hydrogen atoms bond to dangling bonds
2. This effectively eliminates dangling bond defects
3. Results in formation of SiH or SiH₂ units

### Structural Constraints

- Dihedral angle remains substantially unchanged
- Coordination number: ~3.7 (reduced from 4.0)
- Material described as mixture of:
  - Fourfold coordinated atoms
  - Threefold coordinated atoms

### Configuration Types

| Unit | Description |
|------|-------------|
| SiH | Single hydrogen bonded to silicon |
| SiH₂ | Two hydrogens bonded to silicon |
| SiH₃ | Three hydrogens bonded to silicon (less common) |

## Part 2: Donor Impurity Behavior

### Identification Criteria

A foreign atom acts as a **donor** when:

1. It substitutes for an intrinsic lattice atom
2. It comes from an adjacent column of the periodic table
3. It has **higher valency** than the host (e.g., pentavalent in silicon)

### Donor Mechanism

For a pentavalent impurity (e.g., Phosphorus in Silicon):

1. **Valence electron analysis**: Impurity has one extra electron in outer shell
2. **Binding assessment**: Extra electron is loosely bound
3. **Ionization**: Electron easily emitted to conduction band via thermal ionization
4. **Classification**: Atom classified as donor

### Energy Level Characteristics

- Donor energy level (Ed) lies **close to the conduction band**
- Ed assigned to un-ionized donor state
- Small ionization energy required to activate donor

### Energy Level Notation

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Ed | Energy of un-ionized donor |
| Ea | Energy of acceptor |
| Et | Energy of trap |
| Ec | Conduction band edge |

## Output

Provide:
1. Defect/dopant classification
2. Incorporation mechanism description
3. Resulting structural changes (for amorphous materials)
4. Energy level placement (for dopants)
5. Expected electronic behavior
