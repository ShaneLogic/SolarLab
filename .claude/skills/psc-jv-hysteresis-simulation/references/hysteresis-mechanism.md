# Hysteresis and Ion Motion Mechanism

## Key Phenomena

**Slow transient behavior:**
- Timescale: Tens of seconds
- Manifests as hysteresis in J-V curves and current transients
- Distinct from fast charge carrier dynamics (nanosecond to microsecond)

## Proposed Mechanisms

Three mechanisms have been proposed to explain hysteresis:
1. Ferroelectric domains
2. Large-scale trapping of electrons
3. Mobile ions

## Consensus Explanation

The coherent explanation is the **slow motion of positively charged anion vacancies**:
- These vacancies redistribute under applied electric fields
- Redistribution timescale matches observed hysteresis (tens of seconds)
- Creates internal electric fields that modulate charge extraction

## Very Long Timescale Behavior

**Reversible transients over several hours** are attributed to:
- Motion of cation vacancies
- These are much slower than anion vacancy dynamics
- Can affect long-term device efficiency and stability

## Variable Definitions

- **Hysteresis:** Dependence of current on voltage scan direction/history
- **Anion Vacancy:** Positively charged mobile ion vacancy (fast, tens of seconds)
- **Cation Vacancy:** Mobile vacancy affecting long-term efficiency (hours)
