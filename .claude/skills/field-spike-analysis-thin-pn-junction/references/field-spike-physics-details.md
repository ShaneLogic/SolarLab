# Field Spike Physics Details

## Physical Origin of Field Spike

The field spike phenomenon in thin asymmetrically doped pn-junctions arises from:

1. **Asymmetric Doping**: Imbalance between donor and acceptor concentrations creates asymmetric charge distribution

2. **Carrier Injection Under Bias**: Applied bias causes carrier injection that can locally exceed the doping concentration

3. **Space Charge Neutrality Violation**: When n(x) > Na, local space charge neutrality is violated, creating a sharp field gradient

## Mathematical Framework

### Field Spike Condition

The field spike occurs when:
```
n(x) > Na
```

This creates a localized region of high electric field near the junction interface.

### Magnitude Characteristics

- Field spike magnitude can easily exceed 60 kV/cm
- Threshold indicates potential for field-dependent effects

## Impact on Device Behavior

### Potential Distribution Effects

| Condition | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Open circuit | Area under spike is non-negligible |
| Forward/Reverse bias | Minimal influence on overall potential profile |
| All conditions | Slight steepening of Ec(x) near junction interface |

## High-Field Effects Considerations

When field spikes exceed critical thresholds, consider:

1. **Impact Ionization**: Carrier multiplication effects
2. **Velocity Saturation**: Carrier transport limitations
3. **Band-to-Band Tunneling**: Quantum mechanical effects
4. **Hot Carrier Effects**: Non-equilibrium carrier distributions
