# Historical Background

## First Observation

The magneto-phonon resonance effect was first observed by Firsov et al. (1964) in InSb (Indium Antimonide). This pioneering work established the theoretical framework for understanding the interaction between Landau levels and longitudinal optical phonons.

## Key References

- **Gurevich and Firsov (1964)**: Established the method for obtaining information about effective carrier mass through magneto-phonon resonance.
- **Firsov et al. (1964)**: First experimental observation in InSb.

## High-Field Extensions

When higher electric fields are applied in addition to the magnetic field:
- Carrier heating takes place
- Distinct multiphonon transitions can be observed
- This extends the analysis capabilities beyond the basic resonance condition
